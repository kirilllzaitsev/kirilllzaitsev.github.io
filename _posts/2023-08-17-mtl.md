With a [spark](https://arxiv.org/pdf/1810.04805.pdf) in 2018, [foundational models](https://hai.stanford.edu/news/reflections-foundation-models) became a prominent trend in NLP, vision, and reinforcement learning. While large language models (LLMs) dominate the NLP field for years, the vision community started to catch up when vision transformers came into play in 2021 ([link](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2010.11929.pdf)).

Foundational models for vision aim at solving tasks like image classification, object detection, segmentation, and depth estimation all at once via a single model. This approach is called multi-task learning (MTL), and that's what we are going to explore.

Github repository with code: <https://github.com/kirilllzaitsev/vision_mtl>.

## Step 1: Prepare the data

We will use the [Cityscapes](https://www.cityscapes-dataset.com/) dataset for our experiments. There are multiple editions of the dataset, and we need to pick one for the experiments. Created for the [Multi-Task Attention Network](https://arxiv.org/abs/1803.10704) paper, here is how a sample from [the dataset](https://www.kaggle.com/datasets/sakshaymahna/cityscapes-depth-and-segmentation) that we will use looks like:

For the segmentation task, our version of the dataset contains 5000 images with accurate pixel-level annotations for 19 semantic classes : road, sidewalk, building, wall, fence, pole, traffic light, traffic sign, vegetation, terrain, sky, person, rider, car, truck, bus, train, motorcycle, bicycle. These are present in a significant number of autonomous driving scenes.

</figure>
